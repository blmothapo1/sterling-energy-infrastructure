Project Updates

Follow our progress as we build premium EV charging infrastructure across Utah

Proposal submitted to property owner (CRG)

November 1, 2025

We've submitted our formal proposal to CRG, the property owner at Traverse Mountain. This marks the first step toward securing the site for our premium DC fast-charging hub.

Our proposal outlines the infrastructure design, amenities, and partnership structure to deliver a best-in-class charging experience.

Next Steps:

  • Preliminary feedback expected in the coming weeks
  • Site walkthrough and technical assessment
  • Refinement of terms and timeline

Tesla NDA executed—program options in review

October 25, 2025

We've executed a non-disclosure agreement with Tesla to explore participation in their charging partner program. We are now reviewing available options.

Our goal is to ensure compatibility with all EVs while leveraging Tesla's network expertise and technology standards.

Key Considerations:

  • NACS (Tesla) and CCS compatibility
  • Integration with Tesla's network management
  • Uptime and reliability standards alignment

UDOT NEVI clarification: treating Traverse as private/partnership site

October 15, 2025

The Utah Department of Transportation clarified that NEVI funding currently focuses on publicly owned or partnership sites. Our Traverse Mountain hub will be treated as a private/partnership site, shaping our funding strategy accordingly.

We continue to evaluate grants, incentives, and private investment opportunities to deliver best-in-class charging infrastructure.

This clarity allows us to move forward with a financing structure optimized for private-sector efficiency and premium service delivery.

Siting and scoping underway for future locations

October 10, 2025

Beyond Traverse Mountain, we're actively scouting and scoping additional locations in South Jordan and the Orem/Provo corridor.

Our site selection criteria prioritize:

  • High-traffic corridors and destinations
  • Proximity to retail, dining, and services
  • Utility infrastructure capacity
  • Safe, accessible environments

We're excited to expand SEI's network across Utah's Wasatch Front.
