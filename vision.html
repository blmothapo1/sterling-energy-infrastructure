Sterling Energy Infrastructure: Long-Term Vision

SEI is redefining EV charging as a destination. Our roadmap spans 5, 10, 15, and 20 years—focused on sustainable scale, operational excellence, and community impact across the Mountain West and beyond.

5-Year Plan (2025–2030)

  • Launch flagship hub in Draper/Lehi with universal fast charging (NACS + CCS).
  • Achieve site-level profitability in ~3–4 years via high-traffic retail co-locations and operating discipline.
  • Expand to Provo, Orem, and South Jordan.
  • Build brand loyalty with premium amenities (healthy food, workspace, kids area, safety & lighting).
  • Reduce CAPEX using available state rebates and federal 30C tax credits (where eligible).

10-Year Plan (2030–2035)

  • Extend the network across Utah and into Idaho/Nevada along I-15 & I-84.
  • Add solar + onsite battery storage to cut demand peaks and improve uptime.
  • Launch SEI-branded retail/food concepts at select hubs.
  • Roll out fleet programs (rideshare, delivery, logistics) with dedicated bays and billing.
  • Monetize data responsibly (aggregate usage analytics, opt-in advertising).

15-Year Plan (2035–2040)

  • Enter CO, AZ, and NM markets; deepen municipal partnerships.
  • Prepare for autonomous-vehicle charging and smart-grid integrations.
  • Be recognized as a regional leader in safe, reliable, experience-first EV infrastructure.
  • Launch the SEI Foundation to support clean-energy education and workforce training.

20-Year Plan (2040–2045)

  • Operate a nationwide network of destination hubs.
  • Be a top-tier EV infrastructure brand for reliability, safety, and guest experience.
  • Help shape practical EV infrastructure standards and best practices.
  • Expand into Canada and Mexico.
  • Deliver durable, long-horizon returns to investors and community stakeholders.
Utility note: In Lehi, SEI coordinates with Lehi Power for interconnection and service planning.
